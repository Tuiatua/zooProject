The Woodlands

"Woodlands are places where the trees are spaced apart with heath and grassland in between. They often have open grassy
spaces as well as clumps of trees, rocks and bushes."

The woodlands is, for common terms, a forest with rocky terrain and grass and shrubs covering the ground floor.

The oldest found traces of the woodlands trace to the ice age with their soil being a special type of soil.

If the woodlands environment become too dry or warmer, they can quite possibly turn into grass lands or deserts

Depending on where you are in the world, you can expect to see different species living in different habitats in the
woods. Here are some common habitats - dwelling places - within woodlands.

The Moles


are small mammals adapted to a subterranean lifestyle. They have cylindrical bodies, velvety fur, very small,
inconspicuous eyes and ears

moles are animals that have a higher tolerance level to carbon dioxide than most other animals, this enables them to

survive in lower oxygen places and use oxygen better by reusing exhaled air. they also have A extra thumb with different

joints compared to the rest of their fingers. They also mainly live in solitary and only meeting each other for mating,

even if their territories overlap. lastly, their diet, which consists of earthworms and other invertebrates.


The Beaver


Beavers are the second-largest living rodents, after capybaras, weighing up to 50 kg (110 lb). They have stout bodies
with large heads, long chisel-like incisors, brown or gray fur, hand-like front feet, webbed back feet, and tails that
are flat and scaly

Lastly the beaver. They are the second largest rodents and have large skulls with strong chewing muscles.

They can also hold their breath for as long as 15 minutes but mainly only stay under the water for 5 minutes.

Lastly, their diet consists of leaves, bark, twigs, and shrubs.
