The Desert

A desert is a barren area of landscape where little precipitation occurs and, consequently, living conditions
are hostile for plant and animal life. The lack of vegetation exposes the unprotected surface of the ground to denudation.

The Camel


Whenever you think of the desert, the camel is most likely the first thing that comes into your mind. Although disputed between people,

The Camel family (Camelidae) first evolved in North America approximately 44 million years ago during the Eocene
period. Camelops first appeared in the fossil record during the Late Pliocene of North America between 4 to 3 million years ago.

Camels are animals that have a hump on their back, and are mainly used for transportation across the desert.
