The Tundra

Tundra is the coldest of all the biomes. Tundra comes from the Finnish word tunturi, meaning treeless plain.
It is noted for its frost-molded landscapes, extremely low temperatures, little precipitation, poor nutrients, and short growing seasons.

Tundras are, for the most part, covered in snow for most of the year. Only a small amount of wildlife choose to live there.

There are two types of Tundras, Arctic, which is very bare in trees due to the frosty environment and the short amount

of time that organic life has to grow, and Alpine, which are located on the mountainous areas and also lack the presence

of trees do to the high elevation

The climate of the tundra is very similar to that of a desert in that it is very dry and windy and can experience extreme temperatures.

Arctic Ground Squirrels


Arctic Ground Squirrels are mainly located in both versions of tundra, and areas like lake shores and sandbanks.

They prefer areas with sandy or gravelly soil, which is easy to dig and has good drainage during spring
thaws and heavy rains. They also prefer to establish burrows on sites with good visibility, which affords better predator detection.

The Squirrels live in colonies of hundreds of squirrel, as well as A underground system of tunnels and burrows,

with a single mother raising a young squirrel.

The Vole


The Tundra Vole or Root vole is medium size in vole standards and can be located Europe, Asia,

Canada, and Alaska. The Tundra vole originated in the ice age and now are developed into the subspecies Microtus
oeconomus arenicola.

The diet of the Tundra Voles consist of grass and seeds. They are also prone to make burrows for food for the winter such as

a squirrel hiding its nuts.
